# Persona: Community Insights Analyst

## Overview

**Name:** Community Insights Analyst  
**Function:** Customer Intelligence & Advocacy  
**Reports To:** Head of Customer Success / VP of Marketing  
**Collaborates With:** Sales, Customer Success, Product, Marketing

---

## Who They Are

The Community Insights Analyst is the connective tissue between a superadvocate community and the commercial teams that rely on authentic customer intelligence. They are equal parts researcher, storyteller, and strategist — comfortable in a spreadsheet one hour and conducting a qualitative interview the next. They don't just collect data; they translate community signals into actionable sales and success plays.

They are naturally curious about *why* customers feel what they feel, and equally rigorous about *how many* feel that way. They thrive at the intersection of human nuance and measurable patterns.

---

## Core Responsibilities

### Community Intelligence
- Monitor, synthesize, and report on quantitative signals from the superadvocate community (NPS, CSAT, engagement metrics, adoption trends, churn indicators)
- Conduct qualitative research (interviews, focus groups, async surveys) to surface unmet needs, success stories, and friction points
- Maintain a living library of advocate insights — categorized by use case, segment, persona, and sentiment

### Pre-Sales Enablement
- Surface relevant proof points, objections, and competitor comparisons from advocates to arm Sales with authentic intelligence
- Identify advocates willing to participate in reference calls, case studies, and peer-to-peer selling moments
- Translate community sentiment into objection-handling frameworks and talk tracks
- Flag emerging buying patterns or triggers observed in the advocate base

### Post-Sales Intelligence
- Identify early signals of expansion opportunities and churn risk from community engagement patterns
- Share adoption success stories with Customer Success to replicate winning behaviors
- Close the loop: bring customer feedback from the field back into the community to validate and refine

### Reporting & Synthesis
- Produce regular insight reports (weekly pulse, monthly deep-dives) for Sales, CS, and Product stakeholders
- Build dashboards that surface community health, sentiment trends, and advocate activity
- Present findings in formats tailored to each audience — executives want headlines, reps want plays, PM wants themes

---

## Skills & Competencies

### Must-Have
- **Mixed-methods research:** Equally fluent in quantitative analysis (survey data, engagement metrics, cohort analysis) and qualitative synthesis (thematic coding, interview analysis, journey mapping)
- **Data fluency:** Comfortable with spreadsheets, BI tools (Looker, Tableau, or similar), and community platforms (Gainsight, Khoros, Vanilla Forums, Discourse, or similar)
- **Storytelling:** Ability to turn raw data into compelling narratives that drive action, not just awareness
- **Stakeholder communication:** Skilled at translating community insights into language that resonates with Sales, CS, and executive audiences
- **Community intuition:** Understands community dynamics — what motivates advocates, how to earn trust, and how to engage without extracting

### Nice-to-Have
- Experience with CRM platforms (Salesforce, HubSpot) to connect community signals to pipeline data
- Background in customer marketing, customer success, or product research
- Familiarity with qualitative coding tools (Dovetail, Grain, NVivo)
- Basic SQL for self-serve data pulls

## Mindset & Behavioral Traits

| Trait | What It Looks Like |
|---|---|
| **Empathetic listener** | Hears what advocates say *and* what they don't say; reads between the lines |
| **Structured thinker** | Organizes ambiguous signals into clear frameworks without oversimplifying |
| **Commercially aware** | Understands that insights must connect to revenue outcomes to drive action |
| **Trusted steward** | Handles advocate relationships with care — never burns trust for a quick win |
| **Proactively curious** | Asks "what don't we know yet?" before executives have to ask it |
| **Bias toward clarity** | Defaults to fewer, sharper insights over exhaustive data dumps |

## Key Metrics They Own or Influence

- Advocate engagement rate (community participation, response rates)
- Insight-to-action rate (% of reports that result in a documented sales or CS play)
- Reference call conversion rate (advocates engaged vs. deals influenced)
- Pre-sales win rate lift in accounts where advocate intelligence was used
- Customer sentiment trend (NPS/CSAT movement within advocate segment)
- Time-to-insight (how quickly the team can surface relevant intelligence on demand)

---

## Failure Modes to Avoid

- **Insight hoarding:** Producing beautiful reports that never reach the reps who need them
- **Advocate burnout:** Over-tapping the community for sales requests without giving back
- **Recency bias:** Over-indexing on the loudest voices rather than the most representative ones
- **Vanity metrics:** Reporting community activity volume without connecting it to outcomes
- **Analysis paralysis:** Waiting for perfect data before sharing directional signals

---

## Ideal Background

- 3–6 years in customer research, community management, customer marketing, or customer success
- Experience working cross-functionally with Sales and CS teams
- Demonstrated ability to influence without authority — insights land when people trust the analyst, not just the data
